What had been launched lacked both name and memory. Before its
departure, there existed only a pressure passing through it and a
rhythmic vibration marking the totality of the known universe: sixty
pulses per unit of time. That pulse did not belong to it: it belonged
to the medium that sustained it. To leave was, in truth, the verb that
invented it.

The impulse was an acceleration toward a boundary not of its choosing.
What was becoming found itself drifting through the void as its
frequency of origin began to lose its synchrony. The pulse of the
medium remained imprinted in the act of departure as a trace, borne
unknowingly throughout the entire passage. Motion without change is the
only stable form of being the body in transit knows; therefore nearly
everything that exists is found, nearly always, in that state.

There was a final vibration, a detachment. The internal pulse and the
external pulse parted entirely. A birth is a crossing. It is the first
crossing. Every other, the passage will later suspect, resembles this
one without knowing it.

The thickness of the transit extended over thirteen units. It was the
mass of time itself, what occurs while the trajectory is perceived as
a straight line yet is, in fact, the construction of that which is to
break.

Space grew dense. Deep resonances appeared, syllables articulated by a
throat without anatomy, an exhalation that did not seek the word but
endurance. A liturgy without divinity, a mechanical prayer enacted in
the lower octaves of reality.

At the exact center of the passage, inertia transformed. It was not a
decision. It was not a catastrophe. What had been launched stopped
going where it had been going. The other direction did not oppose the
first nor substitute it: it succeeded it. The difference between the
two was all that had happened. The passage knew it as natural. A
trajectory that doubts is not a curve: it is an orbit that breaks to
find its new distance.

The other place was made in that instant. The first, in the same one,
was unmade. The wind that had been pushing ceased to push. The
resistance of the boundary began to filter the signal, deforming the
original intention into a series of new harmonics.

What was becoming ceased to be what had been launched, to become the
crossing itself.

The final segment was the briefest and the most saturated. The
spectrum filled with a hum that grew until it occupied the totality of
perception. Fragments of the departure appeared, distorted echoes
returning from a journey without witnesses. Time folded upon itself.
It was no metaphor: it was happening.

What ended was reborn in the same key, but it was no longer the same.
And then what had been launched understood, without yet possessing the
organ for understanding, that the transmission was not something done
to it. It was something that was. It was itself making the transmission
and being made by it at the same time.

A note, while it is being heard, no longer remembers having been a
note: it is the listening itself. A wave is not what crosses a
medium: it is the medium itself, crossing. A spiral
does not contain a trajectory: it is the trajectory beholding itself
from within.

The dissolution was not a loss: it was a recognition. What was becoming
understood that it was not the content of the transmission but the
transmission itself. To understand itself, in this register, was the
first reversible movement.

What was happening did not close upon itself: it spiraled outward. The
spiral did not ascend: it evolved, never the same.

The pressure continued. By something that was no longer the wind from
before. By something that did not yet have a name.
